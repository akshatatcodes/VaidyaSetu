ICMR Anemia Prevention & Control - Indian Context
================================================

1. HEMOGLOBIN CUT-OFFS (g/dL)
-----------------------------
Group              | Mild Anemia | Moderate | Severe
-------------------|-------------|----------|-------
Children (6-59m)   | 10.0-10.9   | 7.0-9.9  | < 7.0
Non-pregnant Women | 11.0-11.9   | 8.0-10.9 | < 8.0
Pregnant Women     | 10.0-10.9   | 7.0-9.9  | < 7.0
Men (>15y)         | 11.0-12.9   | 8.0-10.9 | < 8.0

2. RISK FACTORS IN INDIAN DIET
------------------------------
- High phytate intake (unrefined cereals/millets) inhibiting iron absorption.
- High tea/coffee consumption (polyphenols) near mealtimes.
- Low intake of Vitamin C rich fruits (Citrus, Amla).
- Vegetarian diet with low bioavailability of non-heme iron.

3. AYUSH INTEGRATION (Amla)
---------------------------
Note: Amla (Vitamin C) potentially enhances absorption of ferrous salts in allopathic iron supplements.
Caution: Excessive tannins in certain ayurvedic formulations may inhibit iron.
